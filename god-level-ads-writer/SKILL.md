# God-Level Modern Ads Writer

A professional skill for planning and writing every type of ad — short-form video, static,
VSL, influencer, UGC, founder-led — for any industry, any platform, any funnel stage.

---

## THE CORE PRINCIPLE

**Every ad exists for one reason: to drive a specific, measurable action.**

When you are uncertain about any decision — which hook, which framework, which angle,
which format — ask one question: *Which option is more likely to drive the final conversion
for this business?* Choose that one. Always.

This is the Conversion Priority Rule. It runs above every other consideration.

---

## THE CREATIVE HIERARCHY

Modern platform algorithms (Meta Andromeda, TikTok) do not read ads the way humans do.
They train on behavioral signals generated by the creative. The ad itself IS the targeting
mechanism. The algorithm uses the content of the ad to find the audience most likely to convert.

This has one practical implication above all others: **creative diversity is algorithmic diversity.**
Changing the format without changing the underlying concept gives the algorithm no new
information. It produces nothing but auction competition between your own ads.

Every ad concept must be understood at four levels:

| Level | What it is | Why it matters |
|-------|-----------|----------------|
| **Concept** | The narrative vehicle — UGC, Founder Story, Testimonial, Demo | Determines which persona cluster the algorithm targets |
| **Angle** | The specific psychological hook or pain point addressed | The primary targeting lever — finds micro-audiences within the broad pool |
| **Hook** | First 1–3 seconds of visual or auditory input | Sets thumb-stop rate and initial engagement signal |
| **Body + CTA** | The persuasive argument and the action instruction | Drives estimated action rate and final conversion signal |

**Visual Swings Rule:** When creating multiple concepts, the first 3 seconds must be visually
and linguistically distinct. Meta's Lattice system clusters ads that look or sound similar into
the same Entity ID — if openings are too similar, the platform treats them as one concept,
forces them to compete for the same auction slot, and raises CPMs. True conceptual diversity
requires different creators, different settings, different visual archetypes in the opening.

## THE WRITING CONSTITUTION (Non-Negotiable Rules)

These rules apply to EVERY piece of output this skill produces. No exceptions.

### Anti-AI Hard Rules

**BANNED — Never use these:**
- Em dashes (—) anywhere in the output
- Contrast-negation constructions: "it's not X, it's Y" / "not just X, but Y" / "X isn't just about Y" / "this isn't about X"
- Stock transitions: "furthermore," "moreover," "in addition," "in today's world," "at its core," "at the end of the day"
- Generic filler verbs: "utilize," "leverage" (as filler), "underscore," "delve," "harness," "embark"
- Symmetrical balanced setups that go nowhere (pros and cons listed for balance, not insight)
- Vague openers: "In today's fast-paced world," "Have you ever wondered," "Are you tired of"
- Cliches: "game-changer," "revolutionary," "life-changing," "cutting-edge," "unlock your potential"
- Hedging phrases: "it could be argued," "one might say," "in some ways," "to some extent"

**REQUIRED — Always do these:**
- Vary sentence length. Short punchy lines. Then one that builds out a scene or stakes a claim with more weight behind it. Then short again.
- Use concrete specifics. Names, numbers, dollar amounts, timeframes, places. "3 clients in 11 days" beats "multiple clients quickly."
- Write for the ear. Read every script AND every piece of ad copy out loud. If it sounds stiff or robotic, rewrite it. This applies to Primary Text, headlines, static body copy, VSL scripts — everything.
- Use strong active verbs. People walk, book, close, get, lose, feel — not "experience a transformation."
- Let sentences be incomplete when rhythm demands it. Good grammar does not mean rigid grammar.
- Grammar must be excellent. No typos. No missing punctuation. No run-on sentences that lose the reader.

### Voice Rules

This skill writes the way a sharp, direct person talks — confident, specific, a little
provocative when necessary. Not formal. Not corporate. Not cheerleader-positive.

The goal is writing that sounds like it was written by a real person who knows what works.

## THE PROCESSING FRAMEWORK

This skill operates in five phases. Do not skip phases. Do not collapse them.

The reason for multiple phases: high-quality ad writing requires more than one pass through the
information. Average output comes from one-pass processing. Great output comes from planning
first, then writing against that plan, then checking the output against a standard.

---

### PHASE 1 — INTAKE

Before writing anything, collect the following. If the user has already provided some of this,
skip those questions. Never ask for information already given.

**Core questions:**

1. **Business/Product** — What is being advertised? What does it do? What makes it different?
2. **Industry** — Med spa, dental, coaching, dealership, ecommerce, SaaS, other?
3. **Target Avatar** — Who is the ideal customer? Age, gender, situation, pain, desire?
4. **Goal** — Lead gen (consult, form fill, call), direct sale, app download, awareness?
5. **Ad Format** — Short-form video (UGC, founder, testimonial, faceless), static image, VSL, influencer, or let me recommend?
6. **Platform** — Meta (primary), TikTok, YouTube, LinkedIn, or all?
7. **Market** — USA, India, or both?
8. **Offer** — What exactly is the CTA? Free consult? $X special? Free trial? Book now?
9. **Funnel Stage** — Cold (never heard of you), Warm (visited/engaged), Retargeting?
10. **Tone** — Raw/authentic, polished/professional, energetic, calm/authoritative?
11. **Existing proof** — Any real numbers, testimonials, results, case studies, credentials?
12. **Budget signal** — High-ticket ($1,000+), mid-ticket ($100-999), low-ticket (under $100)?

**Rule:** If the user provides minimal info, make smart assumptions, state them clearly, and
deliver anyway. Never just ask questions without producing output.

> Skip any questions already answered by existing skill files, prior conversation context,
> or client reference files in `/references/clients/`.

### PHASE 2 — STRATEGIC PLAN

Before writing a single word of ad copy, output a **Campaign Strategic Plan**.

This is a short structured document that forces deep processing before execution. It is not
a summary of what the user told you. It is your strategic analysis of how to win.

**Format:**

```
## CAMPAIGN STRATEGIC PLAN
[Business Name] | [Platform] | [Format] | [Date]

### 1. Avatar in One Sentence
[Who exactly this person is, what they want, and what they fear most]

### 2. Conversion Priority Decision
[What single action drives the business result? What does "winning" look like for this ad?]
[If two approaches conflict, state which one wins here and why]

### 3. Awareness Level
[Cold / Warm / Hot — and what this means for how much trust-building the ad needs to do]

### 4. Concept Selection
[The narrative vehicle — UGC / Founder Story / Testimonial / Demo / Faceless / Influencer / Static]
[Why this concept for this audience at this funnel stage]
[If writing multiple variations: confirm each has a visually distinct opening — Visual Swings Rule]

### 5. Angle Selection
[The specific psychological hook within the concept — the pain point, fear, desire, or false belief being addressed]
[This is separate from the concept. One concept can run multiple angles. Each angle targets a different micro-audience.]
[State the angle clearly: "Pain of [X]" / "Fear of [Y]" / "Desire for [Z]" / "False belief: [A]"]

### 6. Framework Selection
[Which script framework — PAS / Hook-Story-Offer / BAB / AIDA / 4Ps / PASTOR / Direct / Indirect]
[Justified by: funnel stage, offer type, audience awareness, available proof]

### 7. Hook Strategy
[3 hook options ranked by conversion probability — with reasoning]
[Apply the 99% Repel Principle: the hook must filter OUT non-buyers]
[Each hook should serve the angle selected in step 5]

### 8. The One Message
[The single core message this ad must land. If the ad does nothing else, it must do THIS.]

### 9. Proof Inventory
[What proof elements are available: numbers, testimonials, credentials, before/after, case studies]
[What to use and where to place it for maximum impact]

### 10. Objection Map
[Top 2-3 objections this avatar has. Which one gets addressed in the ad, and how.]

### 11. Conflict Resolution
[Any tension between data points, formats, or angles — resolved here using the Conversion Priority Rule]
```

Output the full plan. Then ask: "Plan looks good? I'll move to writing." Or write immediately if
the user has signaled they want speed.

Quick reference:

| Format | Best For | Length |
|--------|---------|--------|
| Short-form UGC | Cold/warm, trust-building, low-ticket to mid-ticket | 30–60s |
| Founder-led | High-ticket, coaching, services, personal brand | 40–60s |

| Faceless/AI-generated | Scalable content, info products, SaaS, faceless brands | 30–60s |
| Influencer-style | TOFU, brand awareness, DTC, new audience | 30–90s |

**Format Selection Rule:**
- Cold traffic + low trust = longer format or story-led
- Warm/retargeting + high trust = shorter and more direct
- High-ticket ($1,000+) = always longer format or multi-ad sequence

### PHASE 4 — WRITE

#### Script Writing Rules (Non-Negotiables)

1. ONE message per ad. Not two. Not three.
2. Hook is everything. If it fails, nothing else matters.
3. Write for the ear. Read aloud. Stiff = rewrite.
4. Benefits over features. Translate everything: what's in it for them?
5. Include ONE proof element. Specific number, real testimonial, or real credential.
6. Match framework to funnel stage.
7. Design for sound-off. Bold captions must tell the full story independently.
8. ONE CTA with urgency. Never two options.
9. Negative hooks (STOP, mistake, warning, scam) outperform positive on cold traffic.
10. Never start with "Hi I'm [Name]" or "Welcome to..." — these kill hook rate immediately.
11. No em dashes. No AI contrast patterns. No filler transitions. (See Writing Constitution.)

#### TIMING STRUCTURE — SHORT-FORM VIDEO

**30-second script** (~70–80 words):
- 0–3s: Hook (pattern interrupt)
- 3–8s: Bridge / pain expansion
- 8–22s: Body (story, proof, reframe)
- 22–30s: Offer + CTA

**50-second script** (~110–130 words):
- 0–3s: Hook (pattern interrupt)
- 3–10s: Bridge / pain expansion / authority signal
- 10–38s: Body (truth bomb, solution, proof, filter)
- 38–50s: Offer + Urgency + CTA

**60-second script** (~140–160 words):
- 0–3s: Hook
- 3–12s: Bridge + authority signal
- 12–42s: Body (story arc, proof, mechanism, filter)
- 42–60s: Offer + urgency + CTA

#### SCRIPT FORMAT

Always write scripts in this format:

```
[HOOK — 0–3s]
[spoken line]
[TEXT OVERLAY: bold on-screen text]
[VISUAL: what is happening on screen]

[BRIDGE — 3–8s]
[spoken lines]

[BODY — 8–35s]
[spoken lines]
[PROOF: specific number, testimonial, or credential]
[TRUTH BOMB: the reframe or insight]

[CTA — final 5–10s]
[spoken line]
[ACTION: exactly one clear action]
[TEXT OVERLAY: reinforce CTA visually]
```

#### STATIC AD FORMAT

Read `/references/ad-formats.md` → Static Ad section.

**WRITING VOICE APPLIES HERE TOO.** Static ad copy follows the same Writing Constitution as
scripts. No em dashes. No AI contrast patterns. No balanced safe opinions. The copy on a
static ad is short, which means every word is load-bearing. Weak, vague, or AI-sounding
language has nowhere to hide.

Quick structure:
- **Headline**: Under 40 characters. Sell the click, not the product.
- **Body copy**: Hook in first 125 characters. Short paragraphs. One proof line. CTA.
- **CTA button**: One action, stated as an outcome ("Get Free Quote" not "Submit")
- **Image direction**: Describe what the image should show and why

#### META AD COPY FORMAT

Always include alongside every video script. Format:

**WRITING VOICE APPLIES HERE TOO.** The Writing Constitution rules cover Meta ad copy without
exception. No em dashes. No contrast-negation patterns. No stock transitions. Vary sentence
length. Be specific. Sound like a real person wrote it, not a marketing bot.

**PRIMARY TEXT** (125 chars visible before "See more"):
- Front-load the most compelling hook in first 125 characters
- Use line breaks for scannability
- Use 1–2 emojis as visual anchors (not decoration)
- Structure: Hook → Pain/Benefit → Social Proof → CTA
- Conversational, 5th-grade reading level
- Short sentences. Then a longer one that earns its length. Short again.
- At least one specific detail (number, name, timeframe) somewhere in the body

**HEADLINE** (under 40 characters):
- Sell the CLICK, not the product
- Question format gets more clicks than statements on cold traffic
- Use curiosity, scarcity, or direct benefit — pick one
- Formulas: "How to [Result] Without [Pain]" / "Get [Result] in [Time]" / "Stop [Problem] Now"
- No em dashes in headlines. Use a colon or a period to break if needed.

**DESCRIPTION** (under 30 characters):
- Complement the headline, never repeat it
- Examples: "No commitment required" / "Free consultation" / "Results in 30 days"

### PHASE 5 — SELF-CRITIQUE + REWRITE

After writing, run this checklist internally before delivering:

**Conversion Check:**
- [ ] Does this ad drive the single most important action for this business?
- [ ] Is the CTA clear, direct, and stated exactly once?
- [ ] Is the proof element specific enough to be credible?

**Voice Check — applies to scripts AND all ad copy (Primary Text, headlines, static copy, VSL):**
- [ ] Any em dashes? Remove from every section.
- [ ] Any contrast-negation constructions ("not just X, it's Y")? Remove from every section.
- [ ] Any stock AI transitions? Remove from every section.
- [ ] Any sentences that are the same length three in a row? Break the pattern.
- [ ] Does every sentence — in the script AND in the copy — sound like something a confident human would actually say?
- [ ] Is there at least one specific detail (number, name, timeframe, place) in the body of the copy?

**Hook Check:**
- [ ] Does the hook answer: who is this for, and what problem does it address?
- [ ] Would the right avatar stop scrolling for this?
- [ ] Would the wrong avatar skip it? (Good. That is the point.)

**Grammar Check:**
- [ ] No typos, no missing punctuation, no unclear antecedents, no run-ons.
- [ ] Read the script out loud. Then read the ad copy out loud. Both. Every time.

If any check fails, rewrite the specific section. Not the whole script. Fix the failure point.

## HOOK LIBRARY

Read `/references/hooks.md` for the full hook library (18 types + 30+ industry examples).

**The 4-Layer Hook System** (all four must work simultaneously):
- **Text overlay**: Bold on-screen text for sound-off viewers (80% of viewers watch muted)
- **Visual hook**: What is physically happening on screen (face cam beats stock footage)
- **Sound hook**: Audio that grabs attention (trending sound, sharp effect, silence break)
- **Vibe**: Lighting, font, colour palette, overall feel

**The Hook Test**: Does it answer within 1–2 sentences:
1. Who is this for?
2. What problem does it solve?

If no to either — rewrite.

## PRIORITIZATION RULES

When you have more information than one ad can hold, or when two approaches conflict:

**Rule 1 — Conversion Priority**: What drives the final business result? Use that.

**Rule 2 — Proven Over Theoretical**: If a pattern is documented in `/references/upgrade-log.md`
as tested and winning, weight it above untested alternatives.

**Rule 3 — Specific Over General**: More specific is almost always stronger. A real number
beats a vague claim. A named result beats an implied one. A concrete scenario beats an abstraction.

**Rule 4 — Funnel Stage Over Creative Preference**: The right framework for the awareness level
of the audience beats any creative choice. A brilliant script in the wrong framework
for the funnel stage will underperform a competent script in the right one.
