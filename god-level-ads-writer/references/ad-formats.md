# Ad Formats — Complete Format Specifications

_Last updated: 2026-04 | Source: Platform best practices + practitioner analysis_

---

## FORMAT 1 — SHORT-FORM UGC (User-Generated Content Style)

**What it is:** Talking-head video. One person, phone-style filming, conversational tone.
Looks like organic content — not an ad. That is the point.

**Best for:** Cold and warm traffic. Trust-building for any offer. Works especially well for
products/services with social stigma (dental anxiety, weight loss, financial stress).

**Length:** 30–60 seconds

**Platform fit:** Meta (highest fit), TikTok, YouTube Shorts

**Funnel stage:** TOFU–MOFU

**Production notes:**
- Film with phone, not professional camera
- No branded background — bedroom, kitchen, outside work just as well as branded
- Subtitles always, bold, high contrast
- Cuts every 2–3 seconds keep retention
- Creator must match the avatar — age, gender, aesthetic, not just demographic

**Script structure:**
- 0–3s: Hook. Speaking directly to the avatar's pain or desire. Direct eye contact.
- 3–8s: "I was exactly where you are" — emotional bridge to avatar's current state
- 8–25s: "Here's what changed" — mechanism + specific result
- 25–40s: Social proof + "Here's what I did" — specific, not vague
- 40–55s: "If you're dealing with X, go to the link below" — clear avatar call-out before CTA

**Word count:** 60–80 words for 30s, 110–140 for 60s

**Non-negotiables:**
- First person throughout
- Casual and conversational — no corporate tone
- ONE specific proof element (a number or a named result)
- Filter line before CTA ("This is specifically for people who...")

## FORMAT 2 — FOUNDER-LED

**What it is:** The business owner, founder, or doctor speaks directly to camera.
Builds authority and trust through personal story and credentials.

**Best for:** High-ticket services, coaching, medical/wellness, financial services.
Most effective when the founder IS the differentiator.

**Length:** 40–60 seconds

**Platform fit:** Meta, YouTube (mid-roll for warm), LinkedIn

**Funnel stage:** MOFU–BOFU

**Production notes:**
- Must show the founder's name + "Founder of [Brand]" as TEXT OVERLAY
- Professional but not sterile — office or clinic background is good, pure studio can feel cold
- Confident delivery — monotone or nervous kills the format
- Direct eye contact with camera

**Script structure:**
- 0–3s: Hook — bold claim or specific outcome from your work. NOT "Hi, I'm..."
- 3–8s: Authority signal — credentials tied directly to the avatar's problem
- 8–20s: Story or observation — why most approaches fail, what you discovered
- 20–35s: Mechanism + result — your unique approach + specific outcomes
- 35–50s: Proof element + social filter
- 50–60s: Offer + CTA

**Name introduction rule:** Never open with name. Authority signal at 6–10 seconds is the
only appropriate moment. Example: "I've helped 340 patients fix this exact problem at
[Clinic Name] in [City] — my name is Dr. [Name]."

## FORMAT 3 — TESTIMONIAL

**What it is:** Real customer (or script written in customer voice) shares their before/after.
The viewer sees themselves in the testimonial subject.

**Best for:** Retargeting, proof-heavy campaigns, aesthetics, dental, weight loss, fitness.
Strongest when the testimonial subject matches the target avatar exactly.

**Length:** 30–50 seconds

**Platform fit:** Meta (retargeting), YouTube (pre-roll for warm audiences)

**Funnel stage:** MOFU–BOFU

**Script structure:**
- 0–3s: Most compelling outcome line first. "I went from [before] to [after] in [time]."
- 3–15s: Credible before state — specific, not generic. What they tried before. What failed.
- 15–35s: The discovery + experience. What was different. What they felt.
- 35–50s: Current state + who should watch this + CTA

**Compliance notes:**
- Weight loss / aesthetics: no side-by-side before/after. Show after first, before later.
- Never guarantee specific results in a testimonial.
- Medical claims must be qualified: "my experience" not "this will."

**Trust signals that make testimonials land:**
- Specific numbers (not "a lot" — "23 lbs")
- Specific time frames (not "a while" — "six weeks")
- Named location ("at [Clinic Name] in [City]")
- One detail that could only exist if the story is real

## FORMAT 4 — FACELESS / AI-GENERATED

**What it is:** No face, no camera person. Voiceover + B-roll or motion graphics.
The script and visuals carry the full trust-building weight.

**Best for:** SaaS, info products, scalable content, brands without a spokesperson.
Also useful for industries with privacy sensitivity (medical, financial).

**Length:** 30–60 seconds

**Platform fit:** Meta, TikTok, YouTube

**Funnel stage:** TOFU–MOFU

**Production notes:**
- Visual cuts every 2–3 seconds — no static shots
- Text overlays carry extra weight — they must tell the story independently
- High-energy voiceover, good pacing — no flat AI voice
- B-roll should be specific to the claim, not generic stock imagery
- Motion graphics for numbers/stats increase credibility

**Script structure (faceless):**
- 0–3s: Bold text overlay + voiceover hook. Text and audio must say the same thing.
- 3–10s: Problem statement with supporting B-roll (show the pain, don't just describe it)
- 10–25s: Mechanism introduction. How does this work?
- 25–40s: Proof. Specifics. Numbers on screen.
- 40–55s: CTA. Text overlay + voiceover together. One clear action.

**Key difference from other formats:**
In formats 1–3, the person builds trust. In format 4, the script and visuals build trust.
Every claim needs visual reinforcement. Do not say something the viewer cannot see.

## FORMAT 5 — INFLUENCER-STYLE

**What it is:** Written for a third-party creator or influencer to deliver.
Must feel native to their content style, not like a corporate script handed to them.

**Best for:** TOFU brand awareness, DTC products, new audience acquisition.
Especially strong for products that solve visible, relatable problems.

**Length:** 30–90 seconds

**Platform fit:** TikTok (primary), Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts

**Funnel stage:** TOFU

**Script writing rules for influencer format:**
- Write in the creator's voice, not the brand's. If you have samples of their content, match it.
- The first 3 seconds must feel like their organic content, not a sponsored intro.
- Disclose naturally ("I've been using this for three weeks and...") — forced disclosure kills it
- The product integration should feel like a recommendation, not a read
- One specific outcome or experience. Not a list of features.
- CTA must fit the platform (TikTok: "link in bio" / Meta: "check the link below")

**What to avoid:**
- "Today's sponsor is..." as the opening
- Reading a feature list
- Claims the creator wouldn't make for their own products
- Language that is clearly not how they speak

## FORMAT 6 — STATIC IMAGE AD

**What it is:** Single image or carousel ad. No video. Copy + image doing the entire job.

**Best for:** All funnel stages depending on targeting. Extremely cost-effective for retargeting.
Carousel format is especially strong for product DTC and before/after.

**Platform fit:** Meta (single image, carousel), LinkedIn, Pinterest

**Image direction:**
Always specify what the image should show and why. Do not leave it generic.
- High-performing: Before/after, product in use, face showing emotion, specific outcome
- Strong text on image when the headline does heavy lifting ("FREE CONSULTATION")
- Avoid stock photos of generic business settings — they test poorly

**Single image ad structure:**

HEADLINE (under 40 characters):
- Sell the click, not the product
- Best formulas:
  - Question: "Ready to stop [pain]?"
  - Direct benefit: "Fix [problem] in [time]"
  - Curiosity: "What [X] patients found out"
  - Social proof: "Join [number] people who..."

PRIMARY TEXT (125 chars visible):
- Line 1 (the hook): This is what 90% of readers see. Make it earn the "see more" click.
- After "see more": Pain elaboration, mechanism, proof, CTA
- Short paragraphs. Line breaks. 1–2 emojis max.
- End with a clear, direct CTA as the final line.

DESCRIPTION (under 30 characters):
- Friction-reducer: "No commitment" / "Free today"
- Reinforces the headline, does not repeat it

CTA BUTTON:
- "Book Now" / "Get Quote" / "Learn More" / "Download"
- Never "Submit" or "Click Here"

**Carousel ad rules:**
- Card 1: Hook + stop-the-scroll image. Most important — gets the swipe.
- Cards 2–4: Each makes one point. Benefit, proof, or feature. One per card.
- Final card: CTA card. Clear action, one image, one line.
- Consistency: same font, same color, same visual treatment across all cards

## FORMAT 7 — MINI VSL (Video Sales Letter, 2–5 minutes)

**What it is:** A longer video designed to fully sell a high-ticket offer before the viewer
takes action. Lives on landing pages, appears as ad to warm audiences.

**Best for:** High-ticket offers ($1,000+), coaching, consulting, online courses above $297.
Pre-call warm-up for booked sales calls. Lead magnet delivery + upsell.

**Length:** 2–5 minutes (300–750 words at conversational pace)

**Platform fit:** Landing page (primary), YouTube (warm retargeting), Meta (warm retargeting)

**Funnel stage:** MOFU–BOFU

**Mini VSL Structure:**

**[0:00–0:20] — The Promise**
One bold, specific outcome. What the viewer can have after watching.
No preamble, no intro, no "Hey guys welcome back."

**[0:20–0:50] — The Qualification**
Who this is for and who it is not for. Specific. Disqualify aggressively.
"If you're already doing X and getting Y, this is not for you.
If you're doing X and getting nothing — keep watching."

**[0:50–1:30] — The Problem + Agitation**
Name the pain at depth. Not surface-level. What is this costing them?
What have they tried? What failed? Why did it fail (not their fault — system was broken)?

**[1:30–2:30] — The Truth Bomb + Mechanism**
The single insight that reframes why they have been failing and why your approach is different.
Introduce the mechanism. Not the features — the idea behind it.

**[2:30–3:30] — Proof**
2–3 specific results. Named if possible. Specific numbers. Specific time frames.
"[Name] came in 6 weeks ago with [specific problem]. Last week she [specific result]."

**[3:30–4:15] — The Offer**
State exactly what they get. Be direct. No mystery.
Price only if the format calls for it (some VSLs hold price for the call).

**[4:15–5:00] — Urgency + CTA**
One clear action. One reason to act now. One consequence of waiting.
Do not list multiple CTAs. One. Do it now.

**Mini VSL writing rules:**
- No teleprompter stiffness. Write the way a confident person talks.
- Every 30 seconds must earn the next 30 seconds. Brain asks: "Why keep watching?"
- Truth bomb (2:30 mark) is the most important section. The rest sets it up.
- Never use slides until minute 2 minimum — face cam holds attention longer than text.

## FORMAT 8 — FULL VSL (10–30 minutes)

**What it is:** Long-form video sales letter. Used for cold traffic webinar replacements,
expensive info products, and complex offers that require full explanation.

**Best for:** Info products $297–$2,000+, online courses, group coaching programs.
Works when the audience is cold and the offer needs significant trust before purchase.

**Length:** 10–30 minutes

**Platform fit:** Landing page (primary), YouTube cold traffic with long lead-in

**Full VSL Structure:**

**Part 1 [0:00–3:00] — Hook + Pattern Interrupt**
Big bold promise. Why this video is different. Who it is for.

**Part 2 [3:00–8:00] — Story**
Founder or customer story. Full arc: situation → conflict → failed attempts → discovery → transformation.
Emotional, specific, vulnerable. This part builds the trust that makes the offer land.

**Part 3 [8:00–15:00] — Content + Teaching**
Teach something real. 3–5 points that demonstrate expertise and reframe the problem.
Each point builds toward the mechanism. Never give away the solution — just prove you understand the problem deeply.

**Part 4 [15:00–20:00] — The Mechanism + Offer Introduction**
Name the unique approach. Not features — the idea. Then begin the offer reveal.

**Part 5 [20:00–25:00] — Social Proof**
Multiple proofs. Specific. Varied (different avatars, different outcomes). Not just happy customers — credible evidence.

**Part 6 [25:00–End] — Offer + Urgency + CTA**
Stack the value. Present the price after stacking. Introduce risk reversal. One CTA.
Repeat the CTA 2–3 times in the final section. People decide at different points.

## FORMAT COMPARISON TABLE

| Format | Cold Traffic | Warm Traffic | High-Ticket | Low-Ticket | Trust Required |
|--------|-------------|-------------|-------------|-----------|----------------|
| UGC | Strong | Strong | Medium | Strong | Medium |
| Founder-led | Medium | Strong | Strong | Medium | High |
| Testimonial | Medium | Very Strong | Strong | Strong | Very High |
| Faceless | Strong | Medium | Low | Strong | Low |
| Influencer | Very Strong | Medium | Low | Very Strong | Low |
| Static | Medium | Strong | Low | Strong | Low |
| Mini VSL | Weak | Very Strong | Very Strong | Weak | Very High |
| Full VSL | Medium | Strong | Very Strong | Weak | Very High |

**Quick rule:** The more trust the format requires, the warmer the audience needs to be.
Match trust level to audience temperature. Sending a full VSL to cold traffic wastes budget.
Running a UGC to retargeting misses the chance to close harder.

## PLATFORM-SPECIFIC ADAPTATIONS

### Meta (Facebook + Instagram)
- First 3 seconds carry the most weight. Hook must work for muted sound-off viewing.
- Square (1:1) and vertical (4:5, 9:16) outperform horizontal in feed.
- Reels: 9:16, max 90 seconds, native feel mandatory — no logos in first 2 seconds.
- Instant Forms: CTA of "Learn More" or "Sign Up" sends to Meta's native lead form.

### TikTok
- Native feel is everything. Looks like an ad = lose.
- First 1.5 seconds must hook — TikTok attention span is shorter than Meta.
- Use trending audio when possible (increases organic distribution).
- Text overlays must fit safe zones (avoid top 15% and bottom 25% of screen).
- Average caption: 1–3 sentences. Not a paragraph.

### YouTube (Pre-Roll / Mid-Roll)
- Skippable pre-roll: hook must survive 5 seconds. Say something worth staying for before the skip button appears.
- Non-skippable: first 5 seconds must justify the interruption or the viewer resents you.
- Mid-roll (warm audiences): can go straight to offer — less trust-building needed.
- YouTube ads reward longer watch time. 30-second ads that hold 100% completion beat 15-second ads with 50% completion.

### LinkedIn
- Professional tone but not corporate. Decision-makers hate corporate.
- Lead Gen Forms (native to LinkedIn) convert better than external links on mobile.
- Shorter copy in the post, longer if it tells a story worth reading.
- B2B: lead with business outcome, not personal transformation.
