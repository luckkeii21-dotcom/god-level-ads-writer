## 1. Med Spa / Aesthetics

### Best-Performing Offers
- $500 discount voucher + free consultation (achieved $7 CPL in testing)
- "First treatment free" or deeply discounted gateway facial ($25–$29)
- 20 units Botox at $189 (specific pricing = more credibility than "affordable Botox")
- $250 off per syringe of filler
- Custom facial + Botox bundle at $275
- Free consultation → in-room upsell model

### Winning Emotional Angles
- **Confidence/identity shift**: "The you that's been hiding deserves to come out"
- **Normalization**: "More people are doing this than you think — and most look completely natural"
- **Reassurance**: Fear of looking "done" is the #1 objection. Address it directly.
- **Affordability reframe**: "More affordable than you think — here's what it actually costs"
- **Longevity**: "Results that last [X] months — not weeks like skincare products"
- **First-timer positioning**: "Most first-timers wish they'd done it sooner"

### Best CTAs for Med Spa
- "Book your free consultation" (strongest for cold traffic)
- "Claim your [offer] — [X] spots left this month"
- "See your results before you commit — start with a free skin analysis"
- India: "WhatsApp karein / Book karo abhi"

### Compliance Critical Notes
- Meta PROHIBITS: Before/after side-by-side photos in weight loss/aesthetics
- Cannot use brand names "Botox" or "Dysport" on some platforms without LegitScript certification — use "smooth fine lines," "relax facial muscles," "anti-wrinkle treatment"
- Show "after" in hook, "before" later in video (minimum 5 seconds apart)
- Use "went from X to Y" — NOT "lost X lbs in Y weeks" (outcome + timeframe = risky)
- No claims about curing or treating medical conditions

### Winning Script Angle Matrix
| Angle | Best Avatar | Funnel Stage |
|-------|------------|-------------|
| First-time reassurance | 30–45F considering Botox | TOFU cold |
| Results comparison | 35–55F who's tried other treatments | MOFU |
| Affordable luxury | Price-conscious, quality-seeking | MOFU |
| Before wedding/event | Occasion-based trigger | TOFU–MOFU |
| Anti-aging science | 45–60F, research-oriented | MOFU |

## 2. Dental

### Best-Performing Offers (Ranked)
1. **$99 New Patient Special** (exam + X-ray + cleaning) — consistent top performer
2. **Free consultation** for high-value services (implants, Invisalign) — highest qualified leads
3. **Teeth whitening special** ($49–$79) — low-barrier entry offer
4. **"No Insurance Needed" special** — removes the largest financial barrier
5. **Dental membership plan** ($29/month) — generated $432K ARR for one practice (800 members)

### Winning Emotional Angles
- **Confidence**: "Smile without covering your mouth" — universal, always works
- **Fear reduction**: "Painless," "comfortable," "gentle" (high dental anxiety in all markets)
- **Social/relationship**: "First impressions. Job interviews. Photos. Your smile matters more than you think."
- **Invisibility**: "Straight teeth without anyone knowing" (adults who want discretion)
- **Cost of waiting**: "The longer you wait, the more expensive it gets"
- **Transformation**: Before/after smile makeover — powerful visual proof

### Winning Script Patterns
- Lead with the EMOTIONAL outcome, not the procedure
- Acknowledge fear proactively — then immediately remove it ("Most patients say it was much easier than they expected")
- Specific numbers ("We've helped 1,200 patients") beat vague claims ("We've helped hundreds")
- Trust signals: Years in practice, number of patients, specific certifications
- For Invisalign: "Discreet" and "no one will know" are the dominant selling points for adults
- For implants: "Permanent solution" and "feels like your natural teeth" close the gap with alternatives

### Compliance
- Do not claim to "cure" or "treat" conditions — use "help," "improve," "address"
- Before/after photos: ensure proper patient consent documented
- Avoid guaranteeing specific results or timeframes

### CTAs for Dental
- "Book your free consultation today"
- "Claim your $99 New Patient Special — limited to [X] new patients this month"
- "See if Invisalign is right for you — free smile assessment"
- India: "Free dental checkup ke liye WhatsApp karein"

## 3. Wellness / Health / Weight Loss

### Meta Compliance — CRITICAL
Meta PROHIBITS in wellness/weight loss:
- Implying personal attributes ("Are you fat?")
- Promoting "unsafe" supplements
- Before/after that implies negative self-perception
- Zoomed-in body parts
- Unrealistic weight loss claims (specific pounds + specific timeframes)

**Compliant reframes:**
- Instead of "Struggling to lose weight?" → "Looking for a personalized approach to health and wellness?"
- Instead of "Burns fat fast" → "Supports healthy metabolism"
- Instead of "Lose 20 lbs in 30 days" → "Transformation stories from real clients"

### Winning Angles by Sub-Niche
**Weight loss (general):** Energy and confidence over scale numbers. "Feel like yourself again." Root cause messaging (hormones, inflammation, gut health) outperforms generic diet messaging.

**IV Therapy:** Immediate benefits ("Instant hydration. Immediate results."). First-time discounts. "Productivity and energy" framing for professionals.

**Hormone therapy / TRT:** Specific symptom targeting (fatigue, weight gain, decreased libido, brain fog). "$99 comprehensive hormone panel" as lead magnet performs well.

**Functional medicine:** Root-cause positioning. "Your doctor treats symptoms. We find the cause." High-intent audience willing to invest.

### Emotional Triggers
- "Feel like yourself again" — identity restoration
- "Have energy for the things that matter" — family, work, life
- "Finally have an answer" — relief from confusion
- "Not just survive — actually feel good" — quality of life angle

## 4. Coaching / Courses / Consulting

### High-Ticket Funnel Structure
Ad → Free Training/Webinar → Application → Sales Call → Sale

**Application-based funnels:** 20–50% conversion rate
**Self-study course funnels:** 1–2% conversion rate
Use the word "apply" — it pre-qualifies leads and increases perceived value.

**The word that converts:** "Apply" beats "Sign up" beats "Register" for high-ticket.

### Winning Emotional Angles
- **Freedom/escape**: From 9–5, from trading time for money, from cap on income
- **Status/identity shift**: "Stop being a freelancer. Start being a CEO."
- **Frustration relief**: "You've tried courses. You've tried coaches. Here's why none of them worked."
- **Aspiration**: The specific life they want (income, travel, time with family)
- **Result velocity**: "In 90 days, not 3 years"

### Proof Standards for Coaching Ads
- Specific client names + specific results beat anonymous testimonials
- Include the "despite" factor: "Even though she had no audience, no followers, no experience..."
- Income claims must comply with FTC: Show results as individual examples, not guarantees. Include disclaimer where required.
- Case study format: Before state → struggle → discovery → result. Named client if possible.

### CTAs for Coaching
- "Apply now — only [X] spots available this month"
- "Watch the free training — link below"
- "Book your free strategy call"
- "Download the free [specific deliverable]"

## 5. Car & Truck Dealerships

### Phoenix Truxx Specific Context
- Used truck dealership in South Amboy, NJ
- 190+ vehicles: Ford, Isuzu, Freightliner
- Custom build/reconditioning shop is the KEY differentiator
- Target buyers: contractors, landscapers, fleet managers, small business owners
- Geographic targeting: NJ + surrounding states

### Winning Angles for Truck Dealerships
- **Work identity**: "Built for people who actually work" — contractors, tradespeople
- **Custom build**: "We don't just sell trucks — we build them for YOUR business"
- **Value/anti-dealership**: "Stop paying big dealership prices. There's a better way in NJ."
- **Reconditioning quality**: "Every truck gets the Phoenix treatment before it hits the lot"
- **Inventory urgency**: "Just came off our custom shop. Won't last the week."
- **Fleet value**: "5 trucks for the price of 3 at other dealers"

### Payment-First Framing
**CRITICAL:** Buyers think in monthly payments, NOT total price.
- "$399/month" beats "$34,500 MSRP" — always lead with payment
- Financing options, $0 down, flexible terms = key conversion drivers
- "Pre-approval in 60 seconds" as lead form hook

### Best Ad Formats for Dealerships
1. **Inventory spotlight** (walk-around video): Salesperson walks around vehicle, highlights 2–3 key features, states monthly payment. Ends with "call us or fill out the form below."
2. **Custom build showcase**: Show a completed custom truck + the business it serves. "We built this for a landscaping company in Middlesex County."
3. **Founder/owner trust**: Owner direct to camera about why they're different from big dealerships.

### Meta Lead Ads vs. Landing Pages
- **Lead Ads win for dealerships**: 50% lower CPL, 200% more leads (Driftrock data)
- Average qualified lead cost: $16–$23
- Always add qualifying questions: "What type of work do you use your truck for?" "How soon are you looking to buy?"

### CTAs for Dealerships
- "Click below to browse our inventory"
- "Get pre-approved in 60 seconds — no obligation"
- "Claim your [weekend-only/month-end] deal before Friday"
- "Call or text [number] — ask for [name]"

### Timing Strategy
- Best launch days: Tuesday–Thursday
- Peak browsing: 6–9 PM
- Monthly sweet spot: Days 20–28 (month-end urgency)
- Season: Spring (pre-summer work season) and September–October (pre-year-end tax buying)

## 7. Herbalife / MLM Health Products

### Two Audiences, Two Completely Different Ads

**Audience 1 — Product buyers (weight loss, energy, nutrition):**
- Lead with transformation stories
- Pain-point angle: "I was exhausted, bloated, and nothing was working — until this"
- Do NOT mention Herbalife in the hook — lead with the result
- Build personal brand, not company brand — differentiation is everything

**Audience 2 — Business opportunity seekers:**
- Lead with personal brand + income story
- "I replaced my corporate salary in 6 months — here's exactly how"
- Do NOT use "get rich quick" language — compliance risk + credibility risk
- Show the lifestyle, not just the income numbers

### Compliance (FTC + Meta)
- NEVER make specific income claims without proper disclosures
- Avoid: "Lose X lbs in Y weeks" (outcome + timeframe)
- Avoid: "Make $5,000/month" as a promise — frame as individual example
- Health claims must be modest and accurate

### Winning Strategy
- Facebook ad for 3-day trial pack: 480 leads at $2.64/lead (tested formula)
- Top distributors generate 3–5 buyer leads per day consistently
- Build landing page + email sequence + webinar for scaling
- Personal brand story is the differentiator — thousands sell the same product
- Document YOUR journey with the products — authenticity beats any ad template

## 8. Universal High-Ticket Principles

For any offer $1,000+, these principles apply across all industries:

### The Hormozi Value Equation
Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood) ÷ (Time Delay × Effort)

To increase conversion: Maximize dream outcome clarity + evidence of likelihood. Minimize perceived time to result + perceived effort required.

**His ad copy formula:** "I help [WHO] get [RESULT] in [TIMEFRAME] without [BIG OBJECTION]"

### Trust Architecture for High-Ticket
High prices = high skepticism. Scripts must build trust in layers:
1. Relatable pain (viewer feels seen)
2. Authority establishment (why you specifically can solve it)
3. Mechanism explanation (what makes your approach different)
4. Social proof with specifics (named clients + specific outcomes)
5. Risk reversal (guarantee that makes saying YES feel safe)

### Risk Reversal Types (ranked by power)
1. "If you don't get [result] in [time], you don't pay" — strongest possible
2. "100% money-back guarantee if not satisfied" — strong, expected
3. "Free session to see if it's right for you before committing" — low-barrier first step
4. "Keep the materials even if you refund" — removes loss aversion

### Application-Based Scarcity (Coaches/Consultants)
"I'm looking for [small number] of [specific avatar] who want [specific result]"
- "3 women" / "5 business owners" / "10 contractors" — feels selective, increases perceived value
- Application creates commitment: once someone applies, they're invested
- Scarcity must be REAL — never fabricate

### Cost of Inaction Framing
"If staying stuck costs you $60K/year, is a $10K solution expensive — or cheap?"
Works by reframing price relative to the cost of doing nothing.
Always calculate and state the annual cost of their current problem.
