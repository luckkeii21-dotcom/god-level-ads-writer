## 1. The Hook Fundamentals

### What a Hook Must Do
- Stop the scroll in the first 2–3 seconds
- Answer within 1–2 sentences: (a) Who is this for? (b) What problem does it solve?
- Filter OUT 99% of viewers — only the right avatar should feel it's for them
- Work with AND without audio

### The 4-Layer Hook System (all must work simultaneously)

| Layer | What It Is | How to Write It |
|-------|-----------|----------------|
| **Text Overlay** | Bold on-screen text | Short, punchy, high contrast. Works on mute. Same or different from spoken word. |
| **Visual Hook** | What's happening on screen | Face cam > stock footage always. Use props, direct eye contact, unexpected action. |
| **Sound Hook** | Audio that grabs attention | Sharp sound effect, silence break, trending audio, unexpected music |
| **Vibe** | Lighting, font, colour, aesthetic feel | Raw/authentic OR polished — commit to one. Mixed signals kill trust. |

**Most important insight:** Changing the VISUAL hook has a bigger performance impact than changing the verbal/text hook. "Best hooks show, don't tell."

### The 99% Repel Principle
- Treat ad hooks differently from content hooks
- Content hooks = go broad (more views = good)
- Ad hooks = must repel non-buyers instantly
- Treating ad hooks like content hooks = #1 reason ads produce low-quality leads

### Two Types of Problem Framing in Hooks
- **Objective:** "Do you have 20 lbs to lose?" / "Are you a dentist generating less than $50K/month?"
- **Emotional:** "Are you exhausted, frustrated, burned out, stuck feeling invisible?"
Both work. Choose based on the avatar's dominant emotional state.

## 2. The 18 Hook Types

### CATEGORY A: JUXTAPOSITION HOOKS

#### Hook 1 — Pure Juxtaposition
**Core principle:** Place two things together that don't logically belong. The gap creates curiosity.
**Rule:** The larger the logical gap between two points, the stronger the open loop.
**Bonus effect:** Creates automatic associations WITHOUT direct claims — bypasses skepticism filter.

Examples:
- "How a homeless person became a millionaire in 18 months"
- "I quit the gym and lost 20 lbs"
- "The all-natural deodorant that outperforms the toxic ones"
- "The $29 facial that gets results like a $500 treatment"
- "How I got 50 dental patients with a $97 ad spend"

Weak version: "What happened to my body after I quit the gym" (viewer assumes weight gain = no curiosity)
Strong version: ALWAYS attach the unexpected positive outcome

#### Hook 2 — Quit/Stopped Doing X and Got Y
**Defies expectation = powerful curiosity**
- "I stopped taking every supplement and lost 30 lbs"
- "I cancelled all my Facebook ads and tripled my leads"
- "We stopped doing consultations and doubled our bookings"

---

### CATEGORY B: INVESTMENT/JOURNEY HOOKS

#### Hook 3 — The Investment Hook ⭐ (Must-Test Every Client)
**What it is:** Explains the time or money invested before finding the solution. Shows failed attempts that led to the discovery.

**Why it works:**
- Targets users who went through same failed attempts — they self-select IN instantly
- Builds trust — if creator's journey mirrors viewer's journey, they trust the product
- Creates strong "this is for me" identification

**How to find the right investment angle:** Look at customer reviews for patterns of failed attempts before they found you.

Examples:
- "I spent 3 years and $8,000 trying every diet before I found this"
- "After 14 supplements, here's the ONE that actually worked"
- "I wasted $40K on courses before learning this one thing"
- "3 failed implant attempts later — here's what finally worked"
- "6 med spas, 4 treatments, and $3,200 later — this is the only one that worked"

### CATEGORY C: FEAR & CURIOSITY HOOKS

#### Hook 4 — The Scam Hook ⭐ (Currently Top-Performing in 2025)
**Use the word "scam"** — typically "I thought this was a scam"

**Why it works:**
- Loss aversion: fear of being cheated — brain wants to resolve if it's at risk
- Curiosity gap: "I thought this was a scam" = unresolved tension; viewer must find out why it wasn't
- Works universally — nearly any product or service can use this

Examples:
- "I thought this was a scam — until I saw my results after 3 weeks"
- "I almost didn't try this because it sounded too good to be true"
- "My wife thought I was being scammed. Here's what happened."
- "Every dentist ad looked like a scam to me. Then I found this one."

**Testing tip:** Take a top-performing video and add scam-hook iteration as a variant — easy, fast, high upside.

#### Hook 5 — Fear/Warning Hook (Ethical Use Only)
"Watch out for these ingredients in your skincare products"
"Three red flags your child has a vitamin deficiency"
"The one thing your dentist won't tell you about whitening"

**Why it works:** Triggers evolutionary protection instinct — subconscious treats potential danger as urgent.

**Ethics rules (CRITICAL):**
- Only use when the threat is REAL, verifiable, and proportionate
- Your product must genuinely address the danger flagged
- NEVER fabricate danger or use panic-inducing language
- Target: LOW-VIGILANCE, LOW-INTENSITY fear — a gentle flag, not terror
- Avoid: "This will kill you," "Emergency," "Before it's too late" (too aggressive = distrust)

### CATEGORY D: IDENTITY & TARGETING HOOKS

#### Hook 6 — The Callout Hook
"If you are [avatar] struggling with [problem], this was made for you"

Pure 99% repel mechanism — combines demographic + problem filter instantly.

Two styles:
- Avatar IS the speaker: First person, no explicit callout needed — appearance does the work
- Creator is NOT the avatar: Must callout explicitly ("Women over 40 struggling with...")

End with: "watch this" / "don't skip this" / "this is for you"

Examples:
- "If you're a NJ contractor who needs a work truck without dealership games..."
- "Ladies 40+ — if you've tried every diet and nothing's working..."
- "Coaches making less than $10K/month — this is exactly why..."
- "If you're scared of the dentist, watch this before your next appointment"

#### Hook 7 — The POV Hook
**Data: 10–15% of ALL top-performing creatives contain "POV"**
**Standard test: include in every new round of creative**

"POV: You've tried every med spa treatment and nothing lasts"
"POV: You hate cold calling but your leads are going cold"
"POV: You're a contractor who needs a truck that can actually handle the job"

Pair with high-intensity trigger words: "POV: You hate [X]" / "POV: Nothing has worked and you're done trying"

#### Hook 8 — The "If You" Hook
Conditional statement triggering immediate self-selection.

"If you're over 40 and your skin doesn't look the way it used to..."
"If you've ever tried Invisalign and stopped halfway through..."
"If you're spending money on ads and getting zero qualified leads..."

**Key:** Test two angles: Demographic ("if you're a woman over 35") AND Problem-solution ("if you've tried every treatment")

### CATEGORY E: CURIOSITY & OPEN LOOP HOOKS

#### Hook 9 — The Give Me Time Hook
Ask for an upfront time commitment.
"Give me 60 seconds and I'll show you exactly how to fix this"
"Watch this for 30 seconds — you'll thank me later"

**Why it works:** Viewers who mentally commit to a specific time frame honour that commitment subconsciously. Results in significantly higher hook AND hold rates.

**Extremely underused in paid ads** — borrowed from YouTube title strategy.

#### Hook 10 — "Why Did No One Tell Me" Hook
Implies viewer has been misled or kept in the dark.

"Why did no one tell me about this treatment sooner?"
"I can't believe no one talks about this in the med spa world"
"Why did it take me 3 years to learn this about running ads?"

**Effect:** Creates cognitive dissonance → forces brain to lock in → positions you as authority with insider knowledge.

**Best for:** Authority-building when you don't have major influencer status yet.

#### Hook 11 — Pattern Interrupt Hook
Shock statement that creates mild alarm.
"This lie is costing women over 45 their energy, confidence, and health"
"Everything you know about truck buying in NJ is wrong"
"The reason your ads fail has nothing to do with your targeting"

**Best paired with:** Physical prop on camera with bold statement written on it (cardboard sign, iPad, printed card). Movement = additional pattern interrupt.

#### Hook 12 — The Question Hook
"What if menopause wasn't actually the reason you're gaining weight?"
"What if you could get Invisalign results without anyone knowing?"
"What if your leads were already in your database — just untapped?"

Gets the viewer to say "yes" or "wait, what?" — both are winning reactions.
**Text overlay:** "The real reason [problem]" — "real reason" triggers same curiosity as "secret"

### CATEGORY F: SOCIAL PROOF & AUTHORITY HOOKS

#### Hook 13 — The Proof Hook (Case Study)
"[Client name] achieved [specific result] while [overcoming difficulty]. Here's how."
"Sarah went from hiding her smile to winning Miss New Jersey. Here's the treatment she used."
"This NJ contractor went from 0 to a fleet of 8 trucks in 18 months — here's how."

**Key:** Shows transformation is POSSIBLE for someone like them.
**Compliance:** "Went from X to Y" is safer than "lost X lbs in Y weeks" — avoid time + outcome combinations.

#### Hook 14 — The Authority Hook
"After helping over 500 women through menopause, here's what actually works — and what doesn't."
"After running ads for 200+ med spas, here's the one thing that kills results every time."

"Here's what works and what doesn't" creates curiosity AND positions as trusted expert simultaneously.
**Text overlay:** "How 500+ [avatar] achieved [result]" — specificity + persona = double targeting

### CATEGORY G: NEGATIVE & MISTAKE HOOKS

#### Hook 15 — The Mistake Hook ⭐ (Consistently Outperforms Positive Hooks)
"The worst thing you can do for your skin after 40 is [common thing they're doing]"
"The biggest mistake NJ truck buyers make (and how to avoid it)"
"STOP sending your leads to a landing page — do this instead"

**Effect:** Targets what people are ALREADY doing and reframes it as a mistake → instant shame/curiosity
**Body language note:** Shake head, use arms — physical expression of "don't do this" amplifies impact.

"STOP" in a hook almost always improves performance. Test it.

#### Hook 16 — The Comparison Hook
"What worked for your skin at 25 won't work now — here's what does"
"What worked for dealerships 5 years ago is killing them today"

**Triggers:** Comparison to past self — one of the most emotionally loaded experiences.
**Text overlay:** "[Avatar]: Stop doing [thing] like you're [younger age]"

---

### CATEGORY H: EMOTIONAL & RELATIONAL HOOKS

#### Hook 17 — The Emotional Hook
Lead with a strong emotional reaction — someone upset, surprised, moved.

"People are actually mad at me for sharing this..."
"I started crying when I saw these results..."
"My patient called me 3 months after her treatment and I couldn't believe what she said..."

**Why it works:** Strong emotional reactions in OTHERS trigger emotional reactions in VIEWERS.
Creates a massive cliffhanger — viewer MUST find out what happened.

#### Hook 18 — The Pain Hook ⭐ (Most Powerful for Service Businesses)
Describe their exact daily experience in visceral, specific detail. End with "Does that sound familiar?"

"You're eating less than ever. Working out harder. But since turning 40, nothing moves. You wake up exhausted. Clothes feel tighter. You've tried everything. Does that sound familiar?"

"You're sending leads to your website. You're running ads. But your phone isn't ringing and your calendar is empty. You've spent thousands on marketing and you don't know what's working. Does that sound familiar?"

**Goal:** Viewer feels genuinely SEEN — "That's exactly me."
**More specific = stronger hook.** Mine customer reviews and Reddit for the exact language they use to describe their pain.
**Visual:** Act out the pain while saying it — facial expressions must match.

## 4. Hook Performance Data

| Hook Type | Avg. Hook Rate Lift | Notes |
|-----------|-------------------|-------|
| Scam Hook | +25–40% | Currently top-performing in 2025 |
| Pain Hook (specific) | +20–35% | Must be hyper-specific to avatar |
| POV Hook | Present in 10–15% of all top ads | Standard test always |
| Investment Hook | +15–30% | Best for trust-building with cold traffic |
| Pattern Interrupt (visual) | Up to +85% watch time | Highest lift but needs strong follow-through |
| Mistake/STOP Hook | Consistently beats positive hooks | Always test negative version |
| Question Hook | +150% CTR vs. statement hooks | Best in headline, strong in video too |

**Baseline benchmarks:**
- Below 20% hook rate: Rewrite hook immediately
- 20–25%: Acceptable
- 25–35%: Strong
- 35–40%: Very strong
- 40%+: Elite — scale immediately

## 5. Hook Testing Strategy

**Always create 5–7 hook variations for every script.**
- Same body + different hooks = efficient testing, minimal production cost
- Hook variation is the highest-leverage and lowest-cost testing variable available

**Multiple hooks strategy:** Layer two hooks simultaneously:
- A verbal/text hook from the list above
- PAIRED with a visual explosion hook (fruit explosion, liquid drip, face cam with prop)
This combination maximizes both hook rate AND hold rate.

**Text overlay testing:**
- Same video + different text overlays = different ads algorithmically
- Test: offer-driven / curiosity / bold statement / question / no text
- This is the most underused and highest-leverage testing variable

**Hook variation types from one script:**
1. Different verbal hook, same body
2. Same verbal hook, different text overlay
3. Same script, different visual opener (prop vs. face cam vs. B-roll)
4. Different person delivering the hook (test avatars)
5. POV version of the same hook
6. Scam/negative version of the same hook
7. Question version of the same hook
