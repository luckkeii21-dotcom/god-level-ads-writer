## 1. PAS — Problem → Agitate → Solution

**Best for:** Pain-point products, SaaS, direct response, dental, wellness, med spa, AI tools
**Funnel stage:** MOFU (Middle of Funnel — problem-aware audience)
**Sweet spot length:** 30–40 seconds

**Structure:**
- **Problem (0–5s):** Name the pain clearly. Who has it, what it feels like.
- **Agitate (5–15s):** Twist the knife. What does this pain COST them? What happens if they don't fix it? What have they already tried that failed?
- **Solution (15–30s):** Present your product/service as the inevitable answer. Don't over-explain HOW — just what it delivers.

**Example (35s SaaS ad):**
> "Still spending 4 hours a day chasing cold leads that never respond? Meanwhile your competitors are booking calls on autopilot. Every hour you waste manually prospecting is revenue walking out the door. [Product] finds, qualifies, and books meetings for you — completely automated. 300+ agencies switched last quarter. Start your free trial — link below."

**Why it works:** Directly activates loss aversion. Agitation phase amplifies existing pain, making the solution feel like relief, not a pitch.

## 2. Hook-Story-Offer (Russell Brunson)

**Best for:** Coaching, info products, founder-led ads, high-ticket services, DTC brands
**Funnel stage:** TOFU–MOFU
**Sweet spot length:** 40–50 seconds

**Structure:**
- **Hook (0–5s):** Curiosity-driven opening. Open a loop the viewer must close.
- **Story (5–35s):** Compressed narrative. Personal or customer story that breaks a "false belief." Include: failed attempts → rock bottom → discovery → transformation. Get vulnerable — emotions, not just facts.
- **Offer (35–50s):** Present the mechanism. Don't reveal HOW — introduce it as "the thing that changed everything." Then state the offer and CTA.

**Key insight (Brunson):** "If something isn't working, it's ALWAYS the hook, the story, or the offer." Diagnose failure at this level.

**Story formula:**
Failed attempt 1 → Failed attempt 2 → Rock bottom moment (emotional) → Discovery of new mechanism → Transformation → "That's why I created [offer]"

**Why it works:** Story bypasses the logical skepticism filter. People don't argue with a narrative the way they argue with claims. The false belief reframe (the "truth bomb") makes the solution feel inevitable.

## 3. BAB — Before → After → Bridge

**Best for:** Transformation products, aesthetics, med spa, dental, weight loss, fitness, coaching
**Funnel stage:** TOFU
**Sweet spot length:** 30–40 seconds

**Structure:**
- **Before (0–8s):** Paint the undesirable current state vividly. Make the viewer feel this is exactly them.
- **After (8–20s):** Show the aspirational future state. Specific, visual, desirable. Not vague — concrete outcomes.
- **Bridge (20–40s):** Position your product/service as the bridge that gets them from Before to After. Include one proof element.

**Works especially well with:** Before/after visual content (skin transformation, smile makeover, body sculpting). The visual IS the ad — the script reinforces it.

**Compliance note:** In weight loss and aesthetics — NEVER show before/after side-by-side. Show "after" in hook, "before" later in video (minimum 5 seconds apart). Use "went from X to Y" not "lost X lbs in Y weeks."

## 4. AIDA — Attention → Interest → Desire → Action

**Best for:** Aspirational products, awareness-stage campaigns, premium services
**Funnel stage:** TOFU
**Sweet spot length:** 40–50 seconds (needs full arc)

**Structure:**
- **Attention (0–3s):** Hook — stop the scroll
- **Interest (3–10s):** Introduce a compelling benefit or provocative idea that earns the next 30 seconds
- **Desire (10–38s):** Build desire through outcomes, transformation stories, aspirational imagery, social proof
- **Action (38–50s):** Clear, urgent CTA

**Why it works:** Full emotional arc. Builds desire before asking for action — essential when targeting people who don't yet know they want what you're selling.

## 5. 4Ps — Problem → Promise → Proof → Push

**Best for:** Products with strong social proof, retargeting, bottom-funnel
**Funnel stage:** BOFU (Bottom of Funnel)
**Sweet spot length:** 40–50 seconds

**Structure:**
- **Problem (0–8s):** Identify the pain — briefly, the viewer already knows it
- **Promise (8–20s):** State exactly what your solution delivers. Specific, measurable outcome.
- **Proof (20–38s):** Evidence. Testimonials, specific client results, numbers, case studies.
- **Push (38–50s):** Urgency + CTA. Why act NOW rather than later?

**Best combined with:** Risk reversal (money-back guarantee, "try for free"). Retargeting audiences who already visited the site or watched previous ads.

## 6. PASTOR

**Best for:** High-ticket coaching, consulting, premium services requiring deep emotional connection
**Funnel stage:** MOFU–BOFU
**Sweet spot length:** 45–50 seconds (can extend to 90s for extremely cold traffic)

**Structure:**
- **Problem (0–5s):** Name the problem deeply — what is it costing them?
- **Amplify (5–12s):** Amplify the pain. What's the worst-case if nothing changes?
- **Story (12–25s):** Customer story or your own story. Specific journey.
- **Transformation (25–35s):** The shift. What changed and why.
- **Offer (35–42s):** Present the mechanism and package.
- **Response (42–50s):** Clear CTA with urgency or scarcity.

**Why it works:** The amplification phase is unique — it makes inaction feel dangerous, which is a stronger motivator than desire for gain. Especially effective in health, financial, and career transformation niches.

## 7. Indirect Ad Formula (Lead Magnet / Free Resource)

**Best for:** B2C coaches, consultants, building email list, brand trust, high-ticket funnels
**When NOT to use:** When your offer is "done-for-you" (teaching them how to do it themselves = incongruent)
**Funnel stage:** TOFU — audience doesn't know you yet

**Formula:** Hook → Story → Offer (lead magnet) → First CTA → Proof → What You'll Learn → Final CTA

**Key principles:**
- You are NOT selling your core offer. You are selling the first step in the funnel.
- Hook must call out the problem + who it's for
- Story: vulnerable, emotional, specific — failed attempts + rock bottom + discovery
- Offer: introduce as NEW MECHANISM. Don't reveal HOW — just that it changed everything.
- First CTA: don't wait until the end — give them a chance to click early
- Proof: 1–2 named case studies with specific results AFTER the first CTA
- Final CTA: straight to camera, mild urgency optional

**Avatar mismatch strategy:** If you're a male coach working with women — have a female client film the story-based ad, then she plugs you as the expert who helped her. You get avatar connection AND authority positioning.

**Why indirect > direct for cold traffic:**
- Higher show rates on sales calls (people arrive knowing, liking, trusting you)
- Higher close rates (value has been exchanged)
- Builds list, community, YouTube, brand simultaneously

## 8. Direct Ad Formula

**Works ONLY when at least ONE of these is true:**
1. You have 50,000+ followers or established brand recognition
2. You serve an extremely specific niche (ultramarathon athletes, pickleball players)
3. Your audience has extremely high pain (knee pain, back pain, IBS, bloating)
4. You have a strong risk reversal ("If it doesn't work, I'll pay YOU $1,000")

**Without one of these four:** direct ads produce cheap leads who don't show, don't buy, don't trust you.

**Short version (30–40s):** Hook → Promise + Risk Reversal → Proof → CTA

**Full version (2–3 minutes):**
Hook → Promise + Risk Reversal → Qualify → Proof → First CTA → Introduce Yourself → Your Story → Your Method → More Proof → Restate Risk Reversal → Final CTA

**Critical:** You pay the same CPM whether the ad is 30 seconds or 3 minutes. If someone is still watching at 90 seconds, sell them harder. Cold audiences need more trust-building time.

**Hook rule for direct ads:** ALWAYS call out avatar explicitly by demographics AND problem in the first sentence. Never rely on appearance alone.
> "Ladies over 50 — if you're struggling to lose weight and don't feel as confident as you used to, watch this video."

## 9. The Second-by-Second 60s Framework

The brain asks every 2–3 seconds: "Why should I keep watching?"
The ad must rotate through: **Pattern Interrupt → Pain → Authority → Proof → Clarity → Urgency**

| Seconds | Lever | Goal | Visuals |
|---------|-------|------|---------|
| 0–3s | Pattern Interrupt | Stop the scroll | Face cam, direct eye contact, big subtitles |
| 3–6s | Pain Expansion | "This is happening to me" | Quick cuts, stressed expressions, problem B-roll |
| 6–10s | Authority Signal | Why trust you? | State credentials with relevance to THEIR problem |
| 10–15s | Truth Bomb | "Aha moment" — reframe false belief | Straight to camera, confident |
| 15–25s | Solution Introduction | Product as natural answer | Introduce AFTER the reframe — never before |
| 25–35s | Learning Outcomes | Clarity on what they get | Bullet points on screen |
| 35–42s | Social Proof | Reduce risk | Testimonials, DM screenshots, comments |
| 42–50s | Audience Filter | Who it IS and IS NOT for | Disqualification builds trust |
| 50–57s | Urgency + Offer | Act NOW | Limited seats, countdown, bonus |
| 57–60s | CTA | One clear action | Arrow animations, button highlight |

**NEVER** start with "Hi guys" or a self-introduction in the first 3 seconds.
The only appropriate moment for brief self-introduction is the Authority Signal (6–10s).

## 10. Framework Selection Guide

**Quick-decision logic:**

```
Is the audience cold (never heard of you)?
  → Use Indirect Formula, Hook-Story-Offer, BAB, or AIDA

Is the audience warm (visited site, engaged with content)?
  → Use PAS, Hook-Story-Offer, or PASTOR

Is the audience hot (retargeting — watched video, abandoned form)?
  → Use 4Ps or Direct Ad Formula with strong risk reversal

Is the offer a transformation (weight loss, aesthetics, smile, fitness)?
  → Lead with BAB

Is the offer high-ticket ($1,000+)?
  → Use PASTOR or Indirect Formula + pre-call VSL

Is there strong social proof available?
  → Lead with 4Ps or proof-first hook on any framework

Is the ad for an AI/tech product?
  → Use PAS or Hook-Story-Offer, lead with outcome not technology

Is the ad for a dealership?
  → Direct Ad Formula, lead with payment ("$399/month") not price
```

**One rule above all:** Match framework to funnel stage. The best script using the wrong framework for the audience's awareness level will underperform a mediocre script using the right one.

## 10. Us vs. Them — Mid-Funnel Differentiation

**Best for:** Solution-aware audiences who already know what they need but have not decided who to buy from
**Funnel stage:** MOF (Middle of Funnel — warm traffic, retargeting, email list)
**Format fit:** Carousel, static image, short-form video (30–40s), email copy
**Status:** `[PROVEN WINNER]` — 2025 performance data confirms MOF gap was underserved

**The context:** Most advertisers over-invested in cold (TOFU) and retargeting (BOFU).
The evaluation stage — where the prospect compares options — was neglected. This created
a wide-open gap for brands willing to address the "why you and not them?" question directly.

**Structure:**

- **Opening (0–5s or headline):** Name the comparison directly. Who is this for and what
  decision are they currently trying to make?
  Example: "Choosing between [Your Category] options? Here's what most people get wrong."

- **The Gap (5–15s or body paragraph 1):** Name the specific limitation of the alternative
  or the conventional approach. Be factual, not petty. Specifics win.
  Example: "Most [competitors/alternatives] give you X, but X doesn't account for Y —
  which is the reason most people see Z problem within 6 months."

- **Your Mechanism (15–30s or body paragraph 2):** Explain your approach and why it
  addresses the gap. This is the differentiator. Not features — the *reason* behind the feature.

- **Proof (30–40s or body paragraph 3):** One specific result tied directly to the
  differentiator. A client who tried the alternative first and then switched works best here.

- **CTA:** Risk-reduced action. At the MOF stage, the prospect is ready to engage but not
  necessarily to buy. "See how it works" / "Compare for yourself" / "Book a 15-min call"
  outperform "Buy now" at this stage.

**Formats this works in:**

*Carousel (strongest format for this framework):*
- Card 1: Hook — the comparison question or the gap
- Card 2: "What most [alternatives] do" — factual, specific
- Card 3: "What we do differently" — the mechanism
- Card 4: Proof — specific result
- Card 5: CTA card

*Static image:*
- Headline names the comparison directly
- Body: one-line gap + one-line differentiator + proof number
- CTA: low-friction action

*Short video:*
- Use PAS structure but make the "problem" the limitation of the alternative, not the
  avatar's raw pain. The avatar's pain is already known — they are past that stage.

**What NOT to do:**
- Do not trash competitors by name without factual basis (compliance risk, brand risk)
- Do not use vague language like "most solutions don't work" — be specific about what
  specifically fails and why
- Do not lead with your story — they are not looking for a story, they are comparing options

**Selection rule addition:**
```
Is the audience solution-aware (knows what they want, choosing between options)?
  → Use Us vs. Them — carousel or short video
  → Lead with differentiation, not problem establishment
  → MOF proof (specific switcher testimonial) outperforms any other proof type here
```
